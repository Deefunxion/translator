## **Reem Al-Riyashi: The Fedayeen of the Modern Era**

Violet Palestinian Woman's Media Platform

Reem Al-Rayashi, a Palestinian woman from Gaza, joined the Izz al-Din al-Qassam Brigades, to join the vanguard of the Palestinian resistance. After consulting with Sheikh Ahmed Yassin - who initially refused to approve her martyrdom operation and instead called on her to raise her children - she took the palm of jihad for the sake of God. She then carried out her operation, which was recorded in Palestinian resistance history.

In this report, Violet has made a special space for the martyred fedayeen Reem Al-Rayashi: her biography, her life, her relationship with those around her, what was said about her, her martyrdom process, her results and her status among the military operations carried out by the Palestinian resistance, as well as the operations carried out by the women of the resistance.

## **Reem Al-Rayashi: The Daughter of Al-Zaytoun Neighborhood**

Reem grew up in the Al-Zaytoun neighborhood in Gaza City. She had 10 siblings; 6 brothers and 4 sisters. She was the eighth born. She excelled in her studies - becoming nearly top of her class in high school - and chose the engineering field to study, but instead she got married. Due to this she postponed her academic studies. However it was before this that her relationship with the Izz al-Din Al-Qassam Brigades began to take shape in high school, as she began to be influenced by resistance.

She would frequent the Mustafa Mosque near her house to memorize the Quran with her friend. Since a young age she carried the Palestinian cause with her. It shook her with pain. She wanted to take revenge for every mother and daughter, and for every massacre committed by the "Israeli" army. She believed in the call to martyrdom and yearned to achieve it. She would compel the Al-Qassam Brigades to approve her martyrdom operation that would make her a hero.

Because the battalions gave priority for these forms of military operations to men, she would ask Sheikh Ahmed Yassin more than once to accept her request. He would tell her instead to raise a family to give Palestine a resisting generation, but she would return repeatedly until Sheikh Yassin would grant her deepest wish. Although she was the wife and mother of two children, Muhammad and Dha, she said: "I love my children, but the love of God is greater," so she was with the instinct of jihad for the sake of God, and she chose to take revenge on the blood of all those who were killed by "Israel."

### **The First Woman Martyrdom in Gaza**

When the occupation had escalated its stranglehold on the mujahideen of the Al-Qassam Brigades during the Second Intifada, restricting their ability to leave Gaza, the brigades decided to utilize its woman members for carrying out a martyrdom operation. The first choice naturally fell on 22-year old Reem Al-Rayashi. Her husband was contacted, who accepted the request, knowing his wife's desire for martyrdom.

On January 14, 2004, Reem went to carry out a fedayeen operation at the Beit Hanoun crossing "Erez." Days before that she inspected the place and examined it well and got to know closely the mechanism

used for the operation. Reem put her explosive belt around her body and wrapped it well around her, grabbed a pair of crutches and set off towards the target. As soon as she passed the barrier she entered the electronic inspection device, which indicated that there were metal pieces with it, so the soldiers began to search her, and asked what she was carrying.

She replied: "a piece of platinum is in my leg and this is the reason for giving the signal from the device," the soldiers took it to the examination and search room, and when she saw a number of soldiers around her, she seized the moment and blew herself up, killing 4 soldiers with her and injuring ten others.

Reem left behind her husband and two children, and on the martyrdom operation, her husband Ziad Awad said: "Reem objected to the weight of the explosive belt of 10 kilograms, and she wanted a larger belt full of explosives to kill as many Zionist soldiers as possible." Ziad says that the last dialogue between her and her husband, was the "she told me to forgive me if I failed you in anything or bothered you!."

Reem Al-Rayashi was not the first on the path of martyrdom operations. She was preceded by Dalal Al-Maghrabi in 1973, and Heba Dragma, who blew herself up in the occupied city of Asqalan in 2003. Hanadi Jardat, who carried out the martyrdom operation that was in the Maksim restaurant in Haifa, killed 23 Israelis, in addition to Darren Muhammad Abu Aisha, Ayat Muhammad Al-Akhras, Adlib Khalil Taqatqa, Heba Azm Daraghma, and many others.

After the success of the operation, the Izz al-Din al-Qassam Brigades, the military wing of Hamas and the Fatah-affiliated Al-Aqsa Martyrs Brigades, announced in a joint statement, which stated: "The first Woman martyrdom of the Martyr Izz al-Din al-Qassam Brigades, the

Mujahedin Al-Qasami, Reem Saleh Al-Rayashi, 22, from the Al-Zaytoun neighborhood of Gaza City, a mother of two children, were presented towards a crowd of enemy soldiers to avenge our children, women and elders, and to avenge the martyrs of Al-Qassam, the Al-Aqsa Brigades in the West Bank and the martyrs of the Al-Quds Brigades."

#### **The Legacy of Al-Rayashi**

Reem left behind a great legacy to her children who were always proud of her, so her daughter Dha said about her: "My mother Reem is a model for the sacrificing and giving Palestinian mother whose memory will remain living in our souls, unique, with a solid will. The love of the homeland and jihad for the sake of God has surpassed the passion of motherhood, so she gave me the most precious thing she could." As for the mother of a friend of Reem Al-Rayashi, who was a witness to her calls to bleed as a martyr, when she was asked her about Reem's on receiving martyrdom and leaving her children, she replied: "God is better than me, and I will tend to them and put them in the deposits of the Merciful."

On the evening of January 14, 2004, Al-Shifa Hospital received the body of the martyr Reem in a black bag. Her body was shattered by the force of the explosion. When her husband saw her, he said: "What my wife did is an honorable work that honors the Islamic nation. She left our children to join the martyrs' knees. She received martyrdom from how much pain she was in when she watched the crimes of the occupation. We are proud of her."
