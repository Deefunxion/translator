# The Axis of Resistance: A Coalition Against Zionism and US-Led Imperialism

![](_page_0_Picture_7.jpeg)

The Axis of Resistance is a coalition formed to combat Zionism and US-led imperialism in West Asia. Comprising Iran, Hezbollah in Lebanon, the Popular Mobilization Forces in Iraq, Ansar Allah in Yemen, and Palestinian resistance factions such as Hamas and Islamic Jihad, this alliance is united by its steadfast commitment to liberate Palestine, safeguard national sovereignty, and expel Zionist, US, and European imperialist forces from the region.

#### Members of the Axis of Resistance

#### 1. Iran

Iran is the backbone of the Axis, serving as its ideological, logistical, and military foundation. Since the 1979 Iranian Revolution, Iran has transformed from a client state of US imperialism into a sovereign power committed to supporting oppressed peoples—especially the Palestinians. Its constitution mandates that Iran reject foreign domination and extend support to all nations struggling for independence. Supreme Leader Imam Sayyid Ali Khamenei stands as one of the most critical voices of the Axis, consistently affirming Iran's commitment to battling US-led imperialism and defending regional sovereignty.

Lieutenant General Qassem Soleimani, commander of the Quds Force of the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC), played an instrumental role in forging and uniting the diverse fronts of the Resistance. Soleimani's mission was to expel US forces from the region and dismantle terrorist groups like ISIS and Al-Qaeda— formed, funded, and armed by the US, Israel, Qatar, and regional Gulf proxies. He protected religious minorities, such as Syrian Christians and Alawites, while also providing critical support to Palestinian fighters in Hamas and Islamic Jihad and coordinated closely with Hezbollah, Ansar Allah, and the Syrian government under Bashar al-Assad. His assassination by US forces in January 2020 was intended to weaken the Resistance but instead galvanized its members and deepened their resolve to oppose imperialism and Zionism.

#### 3. Palestinian Resistance

Hamas and Islamic Jihad stand at the forefront of the Palestinian struggle against Israeli occupation. Palestinian leaders have embraced this alliance wholeheartedly because the Axis of Resistance is the only coalition providing indispensable material and strategic support. Yahya Sinwar, the former head of Hamas, emerged from the dire conditions of refugee life and prolonged imprisonment to become an iconic symbol of perseverance; his martyrdom during the Al-Aqsa Flood operation further emboldened the Palestinian cause.

Equally compelling was Abu Hamza, the spokesperson for Islamic Jihad's Al-Quds Brigades. Before his martyrdom in March 2025, Abu Hamza emphatically declared, "Every honorable person in this world should join the Axis of Resistance [Hamas, Islamic Jihad, Ansar Allah, Hezbollah, Iraqi Islamic Resistance and Iran]," thus unifying resistance rhetoric and reinforcing solidarity. Together, their legacies underline a resolute commitment to liberate Palestinian lands from Zionist occupation and to crush US-led colonialism.

#### 4. Ansar Allah in Yemen

When the world abandoned Yemen and sided with Saudi interests, Hezbollah stepped in; indeed, Hezbollah commander Muhammed Hussein Srour was martyred while training Yemeni Ansar Allah fighters in a tent, a testament to the deep bond and solidarity within the Axis.

Despite relentless daily bombings, Ansar Allah has not ceased its missile operations and naval blockade in unwavering solidarity with Gaza; its rising popularity in Yemen is largely due to its success in achieving true independence and rallying the people against US and Israeli oppression. As the founder Hussein al-Houthi declared, "Everyone in Yemen began to revolt against the US and Israel... Isn't it natural he would gain loyalty, popularity, and support?" This remains evident today as Ansar Allah continues to adopt a disciplined, resolute stance against imperialist forces.

#### **Lessons from History**

History offers numerous examples of colonial powers defeated through armed resistance. Algeria's independence from France in 1962 was the result of an eight-year armed struggle led by the National Liberation Front (FLN). The Vietnamese liberation movement triumphed over French and US imperialism after 30 years of war, culminating in the fall of Saigon in 1975. In South Yemen, the National Liberation Front expelled British colonial forces in 1967 after four years of guerrilla warfare. These victories were not achieved through negotiations or appeals to international law—they were won on the battlefield.

Palestine is no exception. The Zionist regime, backed by US imperialism, will not leave voluntarily. It must be defeated militarily, and the Axis of Resistance has already demonstrated its capability to inflict significant blows on the Zionist project.

#### Armed Struggle is the Only Path Forward

The material accomplishments of the Axis of Resistance underscore an undeniable truth: only armed struggle can dismantle the Zionist regime and liberate Palestine. From the Freedom Tunnel escape of 2021 to the Al-Aqsa Flood operation of 2023, from Hezbollah's precision strikes in southern Lebanon to Yemen's missile campaigns, the Axis has proven that military action is the only language the oppressors understand.

Liberation will be seized, not granted. The Palestinian resistance, supported by the Axis of Resistance, will continue to fight until every inch of Palestine is free. From the river to the sea, Palestine will live forever—not through the benevolence of imperialist institutions, but through the blood, sacrifice, and determination of the resistance fighters and their allies. Long live the resistance! The people will liberate themselves.

#### 5. Popular Mobilization Forces (PMF) in Iraq

The Popular Mobilization Forces-Hashd al-Sha'abi being one of several prominent factions-are a critical element of Iraq's resistance against US-backed terrorism and occupation. Formed in response to the rise of ISIS—an organization armed and supported by US and Gulf state interests—the PMF liberated vast parts of Iraq and forged an unbreakable bond with other resistance movements. A senior Iraqi MP has declared that Hashd al-Sha'abi will expel US forces and restore Irag's sovereignty, underscoring the unyielding unity of the Axis. Their military parades and emphatic "We are with you" messages directed at Palestinian fighters highlight a shared commitment to coordinated operations across the region. This interconnectedness—visible in joint strikes, shared logistical support, and mutual political solidarity—demonstrates that any alliance formed against the Axis will fail. The PMF's robust performance, reinforced by Iranian support and bolstered by Yemeni missile operations, confirms their central role in liberating their nation from occupation and imperialist designs.

The Syrian government under Bashar al-Assad received steadfast support from Hezbollah, which repeatedly defended Syrian territorial integrity and sovereignty against Israeli aggressions.

### Armed Struggle and the Successes of the Axis of Resistance

#### **Palestinian Resistance**

The Palestinian armed struggle reached a watershed moment with Operation Al-Aqsa Flood on October 7, 2023. This unprecedented operation breached the Zionist entity's heavily fortified defenses, captured over 240 settlers and soldiers, and seized military equipment. It shattered the illusion of Israeli military invincibility, exposed cracks in its intelligence systems, and compelled prisoner exchanges, including the release of 240 Palestinian prisoners. These victories were not symbolic—they were material accomplishments that reignited global

solidarity with the Palestinian cause and inflicted severe psychological and strategic damage on the Zionist regime.

The roots of this success lie in a legacy of resistance, from the **Freedom Tunnel escape** of 2021 to the continuous operations by resistance fighters in Jenin, Gaza, and beyond. These actions demonstrate that Palestinian sovereignty will only be achieved by forcing the Zionists off the land through military victory, not through negotiations or international appeals. The leadership of figures like Yahya Sinwar—architect of Al-Aqsa Flood—and the ingenuity of resistance fighters, from paragliders to tunnel networks, underscore the necessity and effectiveness of armed struggle.

#### Hezbollah

Hezbollah, a cornerstone of the Axis of Resistance, has repeatedly delivered crippling blows to the Zionist regime. Just one day after the launch of Al-Aqsa Flood, Hezbollah opened a new front in southern Lebanon, forcing the displacement of over 60,000 Israeli settlers and inflicting significant damage on Zionist military infrastructure. Despite relentless airstrikes, Hezbollah's extensive underground networks and precision missile capabilities remain intact and operational. Its ability to launch long-range strikes deep into occupied Palestine highlights its role as a major deterrent to Israeli aggression.

Hezbollah's actions are rooted in the legacy of its late leader, **Sayyed Hassan Nasrallah**, who famously declared, "When they kill our leaders, we will rise." Even after his assassination in September 2024, Hezbollah continued to strike deep into Israeli territory, targeting critical military and intelligence infrastructure. Nasrallah's vision of resistance lives on, as Hezbollah's military infrastructure remains intact and its fighters remain committed to defending Lebanon and supporting the Palestinian cause.
